# FreeMusic
A collection of instrumental songs for your educational/research videos, podcasts, or whatever you want. Use it, share it, commercialize it, completely free.  I don't care.

I'm tired of always having to search for copyright-free background music for my educational videos, science videos, YouTube content, tutorials, etc. Recently, I acquired a Suno license to try making music with an AI-based tool. After the fiasco with Google's Lyria, Suno's ability to generate complete songs at a reasonable cost surprised me. I spent about three full days playing with the interface and learning some production techniques, but we'll leave that for another day. The point is, when I finally gave up, I still had a huge amount of credits available on the platform. So I've decided to donate them in the simplest way I could think of: producing 100 songs to distribute as open source so you can use them in all your videos, podcasts, or whatever you want. No licenses, no copyright. I don't care.

It might not be the highest quality music production, but I'm using it for my videos, and frankly, the result is very satisfying. Since it's available in a GitHub repository, I'm open to including any other songs you'd like to share in this same format, so that as many people as possible can benefit from it. I believe it's a reasonable way to share resources and use AI-based tools for good. I don't intend to create unfair competition for artists—the production quality of these songs is infinitely worse!—but rather to provide basic content that can help non-experts like myself get by with our amateur videos. There are many alternatives, such as the YouTube Music repository, but they're still backed by a large corporation. Furthermore, their songs are frequently repeated in most YouTube videos.

I hope you find it useful!
